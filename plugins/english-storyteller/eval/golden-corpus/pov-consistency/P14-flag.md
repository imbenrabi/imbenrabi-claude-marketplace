Martinez walked to the wire. His hands were cold inside the gloves. Behind him, the sergeant was watching from the command post — Martinez could feel it, the kind of attention that came with rank.

He reached the northeast corner. The ground was soft here. He crouched and ran his fingers along the concertina. Nothing loose. He kept moving.
