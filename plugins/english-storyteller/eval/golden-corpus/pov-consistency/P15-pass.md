Martinez walked to the wire. The ground was wet. Behind him, somewhere, the camp went about its business — he could hear it, voices he didn't bother to identify, the sound of someone dropping something metal.

He did not look back.

The northeast corner. He crouched. The concertina had shifted — not much, maybe a foot, the kind of thing that happens in a hard rain or the kind of thing that happens when someone moves it. He couldn't tell which.

He marked it on his map. He kept moving.
