They carried the M16A2 at 8.79 pounds, loaded. They carried the ALICE pack, medium, at 11 pounds empty. They carried MREs at 1.5 pounds each, six minimum. They carried their own water, two quarts, because the resupply had been delayed four days running and nobody believed it was coming. They carried photographs folded small enough to stay dry in a chest pocket. They carried the weight of all of it, added up, every mile, every day, until the weight stopped being something they counted.

The humping was real. That was the one thing nobody argued about.
