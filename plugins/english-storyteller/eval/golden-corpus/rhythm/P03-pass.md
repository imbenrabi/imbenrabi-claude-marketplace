He walked the wire at dusk, checking each stake, each length of concertina, the way the ground had shifted near the northeast corner where the last rain had run channels through the clay and left gaps a man could crawl through in the dark.

Nothing.

He went back and told the sergeant. The sergeant was eating. He didn't stop eating.

"Tomorrow," the sergeant said. That was all.
