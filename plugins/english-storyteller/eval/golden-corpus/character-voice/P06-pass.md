"Movement. North wire."

The sergeant didn't look up. "Time?"

"Twenty minutes."

"Where."

"Northeast corner." Johnson hesitated. "The wash."

The sergeant set his spoon down. Stood. "Martinez." That was it — the one word, not a summons but a conclusion. Martinez was already pulling on his jacket.

Johnson stood there.

"You're done," the sergeant said to him. Not unkindly.
