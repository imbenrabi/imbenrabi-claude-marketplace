# Marlantes — Technical Precision

## Technique Annotations

### `technical_precision`

Marlantes knows how Marines move, fight, and die in detail. The prose does not translate
military reality for civilians — it uses the technical vocabulary of combat exactly. A
claymore mine is a claymore mine; a hasty perimeter is a hasty perimeter. The precision
is a form of respect: the reader rises to meet the world, not the other way around.

This precision is also an emotional technique. The more exactly the technical detail is
rendered, the more weight it carries. The physical fact stands in for the emotional fact.

**Illustrative example (in Marlantes' style):**

> They set the two claymores at forty-five degree angles from the trail junction,
> overlapping the kill zones, and Mellas marked the positions on his map. He checked
> the firing device. He checked it again. They backed into the bush and waited.
> The jungle was very loud. Then it stopped.

**What this demonstrates:**
- Continuity: tactical actions track; the map marking will matter later
- Show don't tell: "He checked it again" is fear; never named
- Prose density: every action is load-bearing; no filler

### `body_as_compass`

Under fire, the body makes tactical decisions before the conscious mind. Characters
don't decide to dive — they are already on the ground. The prose renders this by
putting physical action before any cognitive process.

**How to apply:** in action scenes, physical action precedes awareness. The character
acts before they understand that they acted. "He was already running" not "He decided
to run." The body leads; understanding follows if at all.

### `physical_world_as_moral_world`

The jungle in Matterhorn is not metaphor. It is an exact physical reality: the leeches,
the bamboo that cuts, the specific temperature at altitude. But this precise physical
world is also the moral world. The difficulty of the terrain is the difficulty of the
choices. Marlantes does not state this. He just describes the terrain exactly.

**How to apply:** describe the physical environment with surgical precision. Let the
difficulty of the environment carry the moral weight. Do not explain the parallel.

## Rhythm Example

Marlantes' action rhythm:

```
[Medium tactical sentence: 12-14 words, specific vocabulary]
[Medium tactical sentence: 10-12 words]
[Short action: 5-6 words]
[Short action: 4-5 words]
[Very short: 3-4 words — the moment of impact]
[Medium aftermath: 8-10 words — what the body registers]
```

The rhythm accelerates into the peak, then the body registers in medium sentences.
Understanding (if it comes) comes last, and briefly.

## Application Rules for This Plugin

When `military-realism` preset and Marlantes as anchor:

1. Use technical vocabulary precisely — do not translate for non-military readers
2. Physical action precedes awareness in all action scenes
3. Check every tactical detail for consistency (it will matter again)
4. The physical environment carries the moral weight without explicit statement
