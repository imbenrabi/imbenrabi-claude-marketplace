# Powers — Lyrical War Interiority

## Technique Annotations

### `time_dislocation`

The novel opens with the narrator in the present looking back. Past and present are not
separated by chapter breaks but by sentence rhythm: long, lyrical sentences are memory;
short, declarative sentences are the present action. The reader learns to decode the
tense shift through rhythm alone, not through explicit markers.

**Illustrative example (in the Powers style):**

> The war tried to kill us in the spring. Before the trees had done whatever it is trees
> do, before the heat settled in like something that wanted to stay. Later I would think
> about Murph and how he was then, before. But that was later. That morning we were
> just moving.

**How to apply:** use sentence length as the primary time-shift marker. Long sentences
belong to memory and reflection. Short sentences belong to present action. The transition
between registers is the transition between times.

### `earned_abstraction`

Powers earns his abstractions. Before any large statement ("The war tried to kill us")
there is the concrete reality that makes the statement true. The abstraction is the
conclusion the reader has already reached — the author names it at the moment the reader
is ready to hear it named.

**How to apply:** plant the concrete reality first. Let it accumulate. Then — and only
then — state the abstraction the evidence requires. The abstraction must feel like a
discovery, not a claim.

### `lyrical_close_third`

The narrator is inside the protagonist but at a slight, lyrical distance. The prose has
cadence — it reaches for rhythm — but the emotion is suppressed by the military context.
The lyrical voice is the soldier's way of surviving by aestheticizing experience. The
beauty is the damage.

**How to apply:** the third-person narrator has a lyrical register that the character
himself could not produce in direct speech. The gap between the prose's beauty and the
character's terse dialogue is the point.

## Rhythm Example

Powers' characteristic temporal rhythm:

```
[Long lyrical sentence with subordinate clauses: 20-30 words] ← past/memory
[Long lyrical sentence with nature imagery: 18-25 words] ← past/memory
[Short declarative: 5-6 words] ← present
[Short declarative: 4-5 words] ← present
[Fragment: 3 words] ← pure present-tense shock
```

The pattern teaches the reader: long = then; short = now. Once learned, the shift is
automatic. Powers can time-dislocate without any explicit "I remembered" or "years later."

## Application Rules for This Plugin

When `war-interiority` preset and Powers as anchor:

1. Use sentence length to mark time: long sentences = past/memory; short = present action
2. Plant the concrete reality before stating the abstraction
3. The lyrical register belongs to the narrator, not the character; the character speaks tersely
4. Let the gap between prose beauty and character terseness be visible — it's structural meaning
