# Johnson — Body as Compass

## Technique Annotations

### `body_as_compass`

When everything is unreliable — time, memory, causality — the body's physical sensations
are the only facts. Johnson's narrator does not understand what he witnesses. But his body
registers it. His hands shake. He vomits. He keeps moving. The body is the only reliable
narrator when the mind is fractured.

**Excerpt (illustrative, paraphrased from Johnson's style):**

> I didn't know what was happening. The knife was there and then it wasn't. My hands
> were doing something but I couldn't see what. Everything smelled like metal and copper
> and I understood that this was blood, mine or his, it didn't matter.

**How to apply:** in scenes of extreme stress, trauma, or disorientation, abandon interior
understanding. The narrator doesn't know what's happening. His body moves. His senses
receive. That's the entire record. No analysis, no interpretation, no emotional naming.

### `violence_without_comment`

Violence is reported at the same register as everything else. No flinching. No moral
commentary from the narrator. No "it was horrible." The horror is that the narrator
does not react with horror. The flatness is the damage.

**How to apply:** describe violent events in the same tone and sentence length as mundane
events. Do not pause. Do not speed up. Do not editorialize. The violence is a fact.

### `hallucinatory_present_tense`

The narrator is not sure of the order of events. Time is unreliable. Something happened.
Maybe in that order. Maybe not. The uncertainty is not confusion — it is the texture of
trauma. The present tense keeps the reader in the moment even when the narrator cannot.

**How to apply:** in scenes where the character is impaired, traumatized, or in shock:
use present tense (or switch to it); short sentences; acknowledge uncertainty of sequence
("I think this was before that" or simply narrate out of sequence without announcement).

## Rhythm Example

Johnson's characteristic rhythm in crisis:

```
[Short declarative, present tense: 5-6 words]
[Medium observation, flat affect: 8-10 words]
[Fragment: 2-3 words]
[Observation that seems disconnected: 6-8 words]
[Short declarative that re-anchors: 4 words]
```

The rhythm is halting, irregular. It mirrors the fractured consciousness. But each
sentence is internally precise — the flatness is exact, not lazy.

## Application Rules for This Plugin

When `dark-psychological` preset is active and references.json names Johnson as anchor:

1. Use present tense in crisis scenes

3. When the character is in shock or trauma: short sentences, body sensations only,
   no interior understanding
4. Never explain the violence. Name it and continue.
