# Hebrew Register Profiles

Eight register archetypes for character voice design. Each character in a story should be assigned one primary register. Narrator register is separate from character registers.

---

## 1. Ultra-Formal / Literary-Archaic

**Who uses it:** archaic narrators, elderly religious characters, ceremonial moments, ironic distancing.

**Characteristics:**
- Biblical-influenced syntax and vocabulary
- Long clauses, parallel structures
- Uses: בטרם, אשר, כי (as "because"), טרם, אחרי אשר
- Avoids contractions, slang, English loanwords
- Sentence length: long (20–40 words)
- Fragmentation level: none
- Profanity: never

**Example:**
> "בטרם יצאה מן הבית, ביקשה את סליחתו של מי שהיה שם ולא ראתה עוד."

---

## 2. Academic

**Who uses it:** intellectuals in dialogue, inner monologue of educated protagonists, certain narrators.

**Characteristics:**
- Nominal structures ("ביצוע", "הגדרה", "אפיון")
- Hedged language: "ייתכן ש", "ניתן לטעון"
- Uses "כפי שנאמר", "מכאן ש", "לאור האמור"
- No slang, no profanity
- Sentence length: medium-long (15–25 words)
- Fragmentation level: very low

**Example:**
> "ייתכן שמה שחש כלפיה לא ניתן לסיווג כאהבה במובן המקובל של המילה."

---

## 3. Professional / Bureaucratic

**Who uses it:** work-context dialogue, institutional characters, military officers (non-combat).

**Characteristics:**
- "אנחנו רואים ש", "מניסיוננו", "ברמת הביטחון"
- Clear, structured sentences
- Minimal slang; occasional English terms in quotes
- Sentence length: medium (10–18 words)
- Fragmentation level: low

**Example:**
> "לפי מה שאנחנו רואים פה, כדאי לבדוק את זה שוב לפני הפגישה."

---

## 4. Everyday Conversational

**Who uses it:** most contemporary Israeli characters in daily situations.

**Characteristics:**
- Natural connectives: "אז", "בעצם", "כאילו", "נו"
- Contractions: "שיש" not "שיש לו", dropping final vowels in speech
- Some English loanwords in natural positions
- Sentence length: short-medium (6–14 words)
- Fragmentation level: medium — sentences trail off, get interrupted

**Example:**
> "אז בעצם מה שקרה זה שהיא פשוט עזבה. כן. סתם ככה."

---

## 5. Slang-Dense / Urban Israeli

**Who uses it:** young urban Israelis, Tel Aviv characters, tech workers, nightlife characters.

**Characteristics:**
- High-frequency slang: "אחי", "פצצה", "נס", "ממממה", "נשמה"
- English-Hebrew code-switching: "זה לא רלוונטי", "היא super cute"
- Short, fast sentences
- Interruptions natural
- Profanity: medium (depends on context)
- Sentence length: short (4–10 words)
- Fragmentation level: high

**Example:**
> "אחי, היא פשוט עזבה. לאן? מושג. נשמה, זה לא נורמאלי."

---

## 6. Military / Security

**Who uses it:** soldiers, security personnel in operational context, military scene narration.

**Characteristics:**
- Tactical brevity: verbs first, no elaboration
- Military terminology: "כוחות", "נכנסים", "מצב", "יציאה", "מגע"
- No emotional language in operational dialogue
- Silence as communication (no filler)
- Sentence length: very short (3–8 words)
- Fragmentation level: high (incomplete sentences are normal)
- Profanity: common but functional

**Example:**
> "כוחות בפנים. שתיים. נכנסים. שקט."

---

## 7. Street / Mizrahi Informal

**Who uses it:** working-class characters, certain neighborhood settings, characters under stress.

**Characteristics:**
- Arabic-influenced expressions: "יאללה", "ואללה", "חביבי", "פוסה"
- Direct and blunt syntax
- Imperative-heavy in commands
- No hedging, no self-reflection in speech
- Sentence length: short (4–8 words)
- Fragmentation level: high
- Profanity: high

**Example:**
> "יאללה, תעזוב. מה אתה רוצה ממני עכשיו?"

---

## 8. Youth / Internet / Gen-Z

**Who uses it:** teenagers, young adults in digital contexts, characters who grew up post-2005.

**Characteristics:**
- Heavy English-Hebrew mixing: "זה כל כך cringe", "אני literally מתה"
- Ironic use of formal Hebrew for humor
- Self-referential interjections: "וואו", "לא אנטרין", "רציני?"
- Sentence length: very short, sometimes just exclamations
- Fragmentation level: very high
- Written spoken: represents actual speech phonetically

**Example:**
> "רציני? היא literally עשתה את זה. וואו. אני בהלם."

---

## Narrator Register Note

The narrator has its own register separate from all characters. Common narrator registers:
- `intimate-close`: near the protagonist's consciousness, nearly free indirect discourse
- `detached-observer`: camera-like, no interiority, pure external description
- `literary-measured`: controlled prose, occasional metaphor, measured distance
- `ironic-dry`: slight gap between what is described and how it's described

Define the narrator's register in `tone-profile.json` under `narration_distance`.
